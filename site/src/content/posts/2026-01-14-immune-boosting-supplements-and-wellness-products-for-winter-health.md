## Intro

Winter has a way of turning normal people into supplement detectives: one scratchy throat and suddenly you’re squinting at labels like it’s a tax form. And somehow every option is either “mystery powder in a bucket” or priced like it was harvested on the moon. This is for the version of you who just wants an evidence-informed winter routine that’s easy to stick with—no drama, no giant pill organizer cosplay. Below is a short, practical list of vitamin C, vitamin D3, probiotics, and a few herbal/oral-care picks, with notes on dosage, form factor, and when it’s smart to talk to your clinician (hello, interactions).

## How this list was chosen

I picked these with a “will you actually use this in February?” filter, not a 37-tab fantasy-self project.
- Looked for straightforward, evidence-informed staples (vitamin C, D3, probiotics) plus a few popular add-ons (elderberry, echinacea), without pretending one pill does everything.
- Prioritized clear dosage and form factor—capsules, gummies, liquid, and fizzy tabs—so you can match your tolerance, schedule, and travel habits.
- Favored brands that call out quality checks where relevant (e.g., third-party testing, NSF/USP) and avoided anything too vague about what’s inside.
- Included options that fit real winter routines: daily basics (D3/probiotics) and “I’m feeling run-down” convenience (effervescent tablets, tinctures).
- Flagged common practicality issues: higher doses (like D3 5000 IU), added sugars in gummies, and potential interactions—talk to your clinician if you’re unsure.

## Alternatives worth considering

If these picks feel too expensive, too fiddly, or just not your vibe for a winter routine, here are a few sensible swaps.
- **Basic vitamin C (250–500 mg) instead of 1000 mg:** easier on some stomachs, cheaper, and you can still take it consistently without feeling like you’re “doing a lot.”
- **Vitamin D3 at a lower dosage (like 1000–2000 IU):** a simpler daily default if 5000 IU feels like overkill—especially if you already get some sun or a fortified diet (talk to your clinician if you’re unsure).
- **Capsules over gummies:** less sugar, less “oops I ate four,” and usually more compact to store if your counter space is already a supplement graveyard.
- **Skip the fizzy tablets if noise/effort annoys you:** effervescents are fun until you’re clinking a glass at 6 a.m.—a basic tablet/capsule is quieter and faster.
- **Food-first probiotic option:** if probiotic capsules feel pricey or high-commitment, try consistent yogurt/kefir or fermented foods instead (watch for interactions if you’re immunocompromised or on meds).
